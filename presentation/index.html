Jason Eppink

Curator, designer, and prankster.

Jason Eppink likes to make the world a better place one funny step at a time. He’s a 34 year old American designer from Spring, Texas who curates Digital Media at the Museum of the Moving Image in New York City. He graduated from the University of Southern California with a degree in Cinema-Television Production. He curates exhibitions that mainly include interactive art, animated GIFS, video games, timelines and other aspects that revolve around the online community. He creates and collects visuals for his audience to experiance through humor. Eppink’s work includes mischief, transgression, anti-consumerism, participation, wonder, free culture, urban interventions, online pranks and often with for a non-consenting audience. He adds fun, an element of surprise, and sarcasm making the internet come to life. He is also a senior agent with Improv Everywhere, a member of Flux Factory and an actor who appears as Goosey on The Chris Gethard Show.

"Some people who initially inspired me are Rob Cockerham, Charlie Todd, the Graffiti Research Lab, Steve Lambert, and Banksy. Recently I've been digging into prankster history: provocateurs like Abbie Hoffman, Joey Skaggs, Alan Abel, all the way back to trickster mythology. Most recently: Stephen Colbert. I'm not sure how many of those people would appreciate being accused of being artists, though."
